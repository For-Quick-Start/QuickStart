1. Console.log all numbers
    a. from 0 to 9 inclusive
    b. from 1 to 10 inclusive
    c. from 9 to 0 inclusive
    d. from 10 to 1 inclusive
    e. that are even between 1 and 25. Instead of the odd numbers, write "odd"

2. Ask the user to enter an integer between 1 and 100.
Validate your input and repeat asking the user for this input until the user succeeds.

3. Extend exercise 2
Create algorithm that guesses the number entered by the user optimally such that 
we always exclude at least half of the available numbers in the domain 1 to 100.
In each step, console.log the guess and whether the number to be guessed is lower,
higher or equal to the guess.
Example: suppose the user enetered 35. The computer's guesses:
    50 lower (remaining span: 1 to 49)
    25 higher (remaining span: 26 to 49)
    38 lower (remaining span: 26 to 37)
    31 higher (remaining span: 32 to 37)
    35 match

4. Think of a number between 1 and 100.
Perform the same as above in 3, but require the higher and lower directions
be enetered by the user.
